What is U3A

About our U3A

What is U3A

The University of the Third Age is an international movement whose aims are the education and stimulation of mainly retired members of the community—those in their third 'age' of life. It is commonly referred to as U3A. The overall aim the U3A is mutual learning, where members contribute their knowledge and life experiences for the benefit of other.


The U3A movement began at the University of Toulouse, France in 1973, and was subsequently launched in a different form in Cambridge, United Kingdom in 1982. The UK model for the U3A focused on the enrichment of later life, where course were conducted in small interactive groups and members participate in social and sporting activities as well as academic ones. To participate, no qualifications were required and no awards were made.


Adopting the UK model, U3A began in Australia in 1984 in Melbourne. Since then, the U3A movement in Australia has grown to encompass 240 independent groups over all States and Territories. Individual U3A’s often affiliate with State U3A organisations and, the U3A Alliance Australia is a network that represents U3A groups throughout the country.


For more information about U3A’s in Australia see our Links page.


About our U3A

About Nepean Blue Mountains U3A 

What the Nepean Blue Mountains U3A is and how it operates is set out in our Constitution, which defines our U3A as a not for profit, non-political, non-sectarian association providing equal opportunity to make lifelong learning through the experience of U3A a reality for all third agers. You can view the full text of our Constitution, link here.

Our U3A commenced classes in 1990 and in the intervening years has grown substantially; we now offer more than 90 courses to over 1400 members. You can learn a language, paint a picture, study science or astronomy, try your hand at cake decorating, and learn basic computing. There are courses in photography, a scrabble group and sessions where you can improve your chess skills. On the physical side, there are walking groups, dance classes, table tennis and lots more. Our U3A has something for everyone.

Our Courses Handbook has full details about each course offered, including a brief outline, the tutor’s name and contact details and, the time, place and frequency of classes. You can contact the tutor for any clarifications and availability of a place in the course of your choice


The main campus and administrative office is located in the Penrith School of Arts Building, 3 Castlereagh Street, Penrith. The office is manned between 9.00am and 3.00pm, Mondays to Fridays during NSW State School Terms. For full contact details see the Contact page.

Administration

Administration

The administration of our U3A is undertaken by office bearers, committee members and volunteers. The office bearers and committee members are elected each year at our Annual General Meeting where all members are entitled to vote for candidates of their choice. The rules governing how and when our AGM held and how elections for office bearers are conducted is set out in our Constitution; follow this link to view this document.
